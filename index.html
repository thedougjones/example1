Foreword

A note
before you begin

Sewickley is a rare kind of place. A walkable village of coffee shops, galleries and long-standing family businesses, framed by tree-lined streets of Tudor manors, Georgian revivals and early stone estates. Twenty minutes from the airport and twenty-five from downtown — yet private, elevated, and quietly its own.

The homes that follow have been selected for discretion, design sensibility, and walkability. Each is worth a quiet afternoon in person.

Each residence begins with a hero image, is followed by its story, and closes with a short gallery. Read slowly. Linger on what feels right.

With warm regards, Robyn Jones

The Paddock
Sewickley
Residence No. I
Historic Estate

A rare architectural
treasure.

Tucked behind stone walls at the quiet end of a tree-lined street, The Paddock has watched four generations walk past its gate. Original 1908 millwork and a carriage-house studio carry the house's craft through every room. The yard rolls down to a garden wall where, on a clear morning, you can hear the Village below.
